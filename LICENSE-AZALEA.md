# SmarterGPT Covered Tooling - Azalea Health Internal Use License Agreement and Maintainer Covenant

This SmarterGPT Covered Tooling Internal Use License Agreement and Maintainer Covenant ("Agreement") is entered into by and between **Joseph Gustavson / Guffawaffle / SmarterGPT** ("Maintainer") and **Azalea Health** ("Company").

This draft is intended to define Company's internal-use rights in certain Maintainer-owned source-available tooling while preserving Maintainer's ownership and non-Company rights. This Agreement should be reviewed by legal counsel before signature.

## 1. Purpose

Maintainer has independently developed certain source-available tooling, including LexThority, AXF, LexRunner, LexSona, and related non-Lex materials.

Maintainer wishes to make this tooling available to Company for Company's internal business use on generous, permanent terms.

Company wishes to receive clear internal-use rights without requiring ownership transfer of Maintainer's pre-existing upstream projects.

The parties intend this Agreement to:

1. confirm Maintainer's ownership of pre-existing Covered Tooling;
2. grant Company broad, perpetual internal-use rights;
3. allow Company to create and maintain private forks;
4. allow Company to accept future Maintainer-provided updates;
5. protect Company confidential information and Company-specific work;
6. preserve Maintainer's right to continue developing, publishing, licensing, and commercializing Covered Tooling outside Company, subject only to the narrow Direct Competitive EHR/EMR restriction stated in this Agreement;
7. confirm that Lex OSS Core remains separately licensed and is not relicensed or restricted by this Agreement.

## 2. Definitions

"Maintainer" means Joseph Gustavson / Guffawaffle / SmarterGPT.

"Company" means Azalea Health.

"Covered Tooling" means the SmarterGPT source-available tooling identified in the applicable Licensed Snapshot, including, as applicable, LexThority, AXF, LexRunner, LexSona, and related non-Lex documentation, schemas, examples, tests, adapters, configuration files, package materials, and integration materials.

"Covered Tooling" does not include the Lex open-source core project identified in Exhibit B ("Lex OSS Core"), except to the extent a specific Covered Tooling repository contains references, examples, adapters, or integration materials that interact with Lex OSS Core.

"Lex OSS Core" remains separately licensed under its own open-source license. Nothing in this Agreement limits, replaces, narrows, expands, sublicenses, relicenses, or otherwise modifies the license terms applicable to Lex OSS Core.

"Licensed Software" means the Covered Tooling identified in the Licensed Snapshot, together with any Maintainer-Provided Updates that Maintainer makes available to Company under this Agreement.

"Licensed Snapshot" means the specific version of Covered Tooling identified by repository URL, commit hash, signed tag if any, package name if any, public license, and snapshot date in Exhibit A.

"Maintainer-Provided Updates" means future versions, improvements, fixes, documentation updates, schemas, examples, tests, refactors, adapters, configuration materials, or related non-Lex materials that Maintainer makes available to Company after the Licensed Snapshot.

"Company-Specific Materials" means Company confidential information, Company customer data, PHI, regulated data, credentials, internal source code, internal architecture, private implementation details, proprietary workflows, deployment configuration, security-sensitive information, non-public business processes, and modifications created specifically for Company's internal systems or operations.

"Direct Competitive EHR/EMR Provider" means a company or business unit whose primary commercial product or service is a competing electronic health record or electronic medical record system substantially similar to, or directly competitive with, Company's EHR/EMR products or services.

## 3. Pre-Existing Independent Work

Maintainer represents that the pre-existing Covered Tooling identified in Exhibit A was independently developed before Company adoption, use, modification, or contribution.

Maintainer represents that, to the best of Maintainer's knowledge, the pre-existing Covered Tooling identified in Exhibit A was created outside Company work time, outside Company equipment, outside Company systems, and without Company confidential information, Company customer data, PHI, internal source code, private documentation, credentials, or trade secrets.

The parties may attach provenance materials, signed tags, commit history, repository history, package history, notes, or other records as Exhibit C.

## 4. Maintainer-Provided Updates

Maintainer-Provided Updates may include:

1. improvements authored by Maintainer independently and outside Company work time, Company equipment, Company systems, and Company confidential information;
2. improvements authored by Maintainer and otherwise lawfully owned or controlled by Maintainer;
3. third-party contributions that are lawfully incorporated into the applicable upstream Covered Tooling project under terms that permit Maintainer to provide them to Company for Company's internal use.

Maintainer-Provided Updates do not include Company private fork changes, Company confidential information, Company customer data, PHI, credentials, internal source code, private architecture, proprietary workflows, or Company-Specific Materials unless Company separately agrees in writing.

Company is not required to accept any Maintainer-Provided Update.

Public release of a Maintainer-Provided Update does not automatically modify Company's private fork or require Company to adopt that update.

Maintainer is not required to provide maintenance, support, hosted services, professional services, implementation services, or future updates unless separately agreed in writing.

## 5. License Grant to Company

Maintainer grants Company a perpetual, irrevocable, worldwide, royalty-free, non-exclusive, fully paid-up license to use, copy, install, run, modify, adapt, prepare derivative works of, test, evaluate, deploy, operate, and maintain the Licensed Software for Company's internal business purposes.

This license includes the right for Company to create, maintain, modify, and operate private forks of the Licensed Software.

This license applies to the Licensed Snapshot and to any Maintainer-Provided Updates that Company elects to accept or incorporate.

This license survives any change in Maintainer's employment status, contractor status, vendor status, or other relationship with Company.

## 6. Company Contractors, Vendors, and Service Providers

Company may allow its employees, contractors, vendors, auditors, consultants, professional service providers, and agents to access and use the Licensed Software solely as necessary to support Company's internal business use.

Company is responsible for ensuring that such parties are bound by confidentiality and use restrictions at least as protective as those applicable to Company's own confidential materials.

Company may not authorize such parties to use the Licensed Software for their own independent commercial purposes.

## 7. Company Private Forks and Company-Specific Materials

Company may modify its private forks of the Licensed Software for Company internal business use.

Company owns or controls Company-Specific Materials and Company private fork modifications created by Company personnel within the scope of Company work, to the extent provided by applicable law and Company agreements.

Company private fork changes are not upstream contributions unless Company separately submits them under a written contribution agreement.

Maintainer will not copy, publish, reuse, commercialize, sublicense, or provide Company private fork modifications or Company-Specific Materials to third parties unless Company separately agrees in writing.

Company is not required to contribute private fork changes back to any upstream Covered Tooling project.

## 8. No Required Contribution Back

Company has no obligation to contribute modifications, improvements, fixes, documentation, tests, adapters, configurations, workflows, or other materials back to Maintainer or to any upstream Covered Tooling project.

Company may keep all Company private fork changes private.

Any future upstream contribution from Company should be governed by a separate written contribution agreement.

## 9. Maintainer's Continuing Rights

Maintainer retains ownership of the pre-existing upstream Covered Tooling and all Maintainer-owned future upstream improvements.

Company's license does not restrict Maintainer from continuing to develop, publish, license, sell, commercialize, or otherwise provide Covered Tooling or related materials to other individuals, companies, or organizations, subject only to the narrow "No Direct Competitive EHR/EMR Licensing" covenant in this Agreement.

Maintainer's continuing rights include the right to license, sell, publish, commercialize, or otherwise provide Covered Tooling or related upstream materials to non-EHR/EMR companies, general-purpose technology providers, AI companies, cloud providers, developer-platform companies, infrastructure companies, identity providers, security companies, data-platform companies, consulting companies, hospitals, clinics, health systems, payers, life-sciences companies, medical-device companies, healthcare-adjacent organizations, and other organizations whose primary intended use is not the development, operation, enhancement, commercialization, or provision of a competing EHR/EMR product.

Maintainer may license Covered Tooling or related upstream materials to third parties, including large technology companies, provided Maintainer does not:

1. directly license Covered Tooling to a Direct Competitive EHR/EMR Provider for competing EHR/EMR product use;
2. disclose or reuse Company confidential information;
3. disclose or reuse Company private fork modifications;
4. disclose or reuse Company customer data, PHI, credentials, internal source code, private architecture, proprietary workflows, or other Company-Specific Materials.

Nothing in this Agreement gives Company ownership of Maintainer's upstream Covered Tooling, future Maintainer-owned improvements, public repositories, package names, trademarks, domains, project names, or non-Company commercialization rights.

## 10. No Direct Competitive EHR/EMR Licensing

As a Company-protective covenant, Maintainer agrees that Maintainer will not knowingly directly license the Covered Tooling to a Direct Competitive EHR/EMR Provider for use in developing, operating, enhancing, commercializing, or providing a competing electronic health record or electronic medical record product.

"Direct Competitive EHR/EMR Provider" means a company or business unit whose primary commercial product or service is a competing electronic health record or electronic medical record system substantially similar to, or directly competitive with, Company's EHR/EMR products or services.

"Direct Competitive EHR/EMR Provider" does not include general-purpose technology companies, cloud providers, AI companies, developer-tool companies, infrastructure providers, identity providers, security companies, data-platform companies, consulting companies, hospitals, clinics, health systems, payers, life-sciences companies, medical-device companies, healthcare-adjacent organizations, or other entities merely because they operate in, sell to, integrate with, or support the healthcare industry.

This restriction applies only to a direct license by Maintainer of Covered Tooling to a Direct Competitive EHR/EMR Provider for use in developing, operating, enhancing, commercializing, or providing a competing EHR/EMR product.

This restriction does not limit Maintainer's right to license, sell, publish, commercialize, or otherwise provide Covered Tooling or related upstream materials to non-EHR/EMR companies, general-purpose technology providers, AI companies, cloud providers, developer-platform companies, infrastructure companies, or other organizations whose primary intended use is not the development, operation, enhancement, commercialization, or provision of a competing EHR/EMR product.

This restriction does not apply to Lex OSS Core, which remains governed by its separate open-source license.

This restriction does not prohibit Maintainer from using general knowledge, skill, experience, ideas, concepts, methods, know-how, or independently developed materials, provided Maintainer does not disclose or reuse Company confidential information, Company private fork modifications, Company customer data, PHI, credentials, internal source code, private architecture, proprietary workflows, or other Company-Specific Materials.

## 11. Public Source-Available License

Maintainer may make Covered Tooling publicly visible under a source-available personal-use license, including the SmarterGPT Source-Available Personal Use License or a successor source-available license.

Public availability of Covered Tooling does not grant Company rights beyond the rights expressly granted in this Agreement, and does not convert Covered Tooling into open-source software unless Maintainer separately applies an open-source license to that specific project.

Company's rights to use the Licensed Software for internal business purposes arise from this Agreement, not from the public source-available license.

The public license for Covered Tooling does not apply to Company-Specific Materials, Company private fork modifications, Company confidential information, Company customer data, PHI, credentials, internal source code, private architecture, proprietary workflows, or security-sensitive information.

## 12. Restrictions on Company

Company may not sell, resell, sublicense, distribute, commercialize, host, or offer the Licensed Software as a standalone product, hosted service, managed service, platform feature, SDK, API, external service, or commercial service to third parties without a separate written agreement.

Company may use the Licensed Software internally to support Company business operations, internal engineering workflows, internal agent workflows, internal development processes, internal automation, and internal governance.

Company may not use the Licensed Software in a way that implies Maintainer endorsement of Company products or services without Maintainer's prior written consent.

## 13. Confidentiality and Regulated Data

No Company confidential information, PHI, customer data, credentials, internal URLs, security-sensitive information, internal source code, private architecture, or proprietary workflows should be contributed to any public upstream Covered Tooling repository.

Any public discussion, publication, issue, pull request, or upstream contribution involving Covered Tooling should be reviewed to ensure it does not disclose Company confidential or regulated information.

Maintainer will not knowingly include Company-Specific Materials in public Covered Tooling.

## 14. Third-Party Contributions and Dependencies

Maintainer may incorporate third-party contributions or dependencies into upstream Covered Tooling only to the extent permitted by their applicable licenses or contribution terms.

Company's rights to use any third-party contribution or dependency are subject to the applicable third-party license terms.

## 15. No Transfer of Upstream Ownership

Company's use of, copying of, modification of, access to, or private forking of the Licensed Software does not transfer ownership of the pre-existing upstream Covered Tooling to Company.

No copyright ownership or exclusive rights are transferred to Company except through an express written assignment signed by Maintainer.

Ownership of a private copy, private fork, or Company-specific modification does not by itself transfer ownership of Maintainer's upstream copyrights or other intellectual property rights.
